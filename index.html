02

Quantify

Now that you have completed the first exercise, it is time to measure your thoughts. your thoughts. Let's learn how to decrease the amount of them.

At many moments while we are talking, our mind is agitated. This will help you calm your mind and decrease the amount of thoughts. thoughts.

Exercise:
For 1 minute, be silent and focus on your breathing. Count how many thoughts go through your mind. Write down each of them on a sheet of paper. Repeat the exercise a few times if necessary. necessary.

03

Relax

Not all feedback will be positive. Not all will be negative. The important thing is that their existence can help you evolve.

We are always subject to objections and different judgments within communication. Noise exists, but the intention of your speech should be to convey knowledge be to transmit knowledge and not to seek the acceptance of all. everyone. We are not unanimous, and that's fine.

To keep in mind:
Focus on the content and the message you want to get across. Don't be bother if you get negative feedbacks. There will always be room for evolution.

04

Encourage yourself

Fear exists. Let's accept it. We can, with some practice, learn to live with it with it without it annulling us.

When it comes to communicating, we often fear debate, judgment, or not being clear. of debate, of judgment, or of not being clear. Fear is part of our nature nature and manifests itself to protect us. But in the case of communication, it can prevent us from connecting with others.

Write it down somewhere:
Write down your fear and reflect on it. Recognize the reason for its existence and respect it. You have to accept it in order to control it.

05

Understand

Knowing how to evaluate the emotions of the other is useful to give us sensitivity, to analyze and to perceive the emotions of the other.

With empathy, we can adjust our communication for a better understanding understanding of those listening to us, decreasing the possibility of noise.

To keep in mind:
Observe without judging, with a focus on people; recognize the emotions of the other and show respect about these emotions and opinions.

06

Trust

Time to look inside. To our thoughts and to what holds us back and evolve.

Working on trust is knowing how to deal with emotions and not being fear of judgments. What are your beliefs about what you can or cannot do? What keeps you from communicating better better today? The intention is to evaluate your limitations and transform them into positive points.

Take note:
1. Reflect on your limitations in communication and write them down.
2. Cross them out and next to them write the opposite (ex: I am not able to ...)
3. Always review this positive side.

07

Appoint yourself

The body also speaks. Proper posture helps to convey more confidence.

Just as a chin positioned a few inches up can convey superiority, a smile can make you friendly and approachable. Always be aware of your posture and your and your facial expression. In presentation if you are videoconferencing, be careful that your chair does not fall back.

Note somewhere:

Spine straight, shoulders back and chest forward.
Note: doing physical exercises helps work on posture.

08

Express

Having a more impactful communication achieves the goal of gain the attention of those listening to us.

There is no magic formula for how to present ideas but, there are ways to make them better. Be more expressive (more theatrical). Work on your facial expression, your tone of voice and your gestures to enhance your message. Show animation.

To practice:
1. choose a story.
2. Depict the situations involved.
3. Be expressive.

09

Plan yourself

It is possible to have planning to better structure and transmit the knowledge so that speaking is not just improvisation.

Start by studying your target audience, for whom your message. Ask questions, give examples, be specific and present evidence, facts, analogies, testimonials or statistics. They will give more credibility than your own opinions.

Tip:
During your presentation, try to measure the level of engagement of the people by asking questions. Besides being a great interaction, it encourages reflection and focus on your speech.

10

Discurse

Finally, knowing how to validate your ideas with a beginning middle and end is essential to facilitate the exchange of knowledge or any information.

Very handy, this feature can be used whenever you need to defend a point of view. Here it is important to to emphasize what the problem to be solved is, and, above all, to come up with to a solution.

You can structure your speech into 4 moments:
1. Opening - Get your listener's attention by asking questions or statements;
2. Problematizing - Bringing up a need (pain) and make him/her aware of it.
3. Solution - Solving the problem.
4. Conclusion - Recognize the benefit of your solution.
